A weary woman winds
Her polished pocket watch
The regulars arrive
Their distanced smiles and nods

A train creeps down the track
One hesitates to follow
She grabs his hand, a pact
To think and wish and wallow

Behind him cowers a schoolgirl
Too late to leave this train
She punched her ticket in a whirl
When she ate pills to mask her pain

A veteran stares out his window
Seeing friends from better days
He decided his wife a widow
And boarded this train with sealed fate

"All aboard," the conductor calls
To people with no answers
On and on the engine drawls
For them there is no transfer
