The MS gate entangles ion qubits by driving a shared motional mode around a closed, spin-dependent loop so the motion returns to its initial state while a nontrivial two-qubit phase remains.

## Intuitive explanation
Two ions share a vibrational mode. Drive both ions with two laser tones near the first red and blue sidebands (spin flip with $\pm 1$ phonon), detuned so phonons are only virtual. The combined drive acts as a spin-dependent force, displacing the shared mode in phase space by an amount set by the joint spin state. Choose the detuning and pulse time so this displacement traces a closed loop: motion returns, while the loop's geometric phase remains as an XX (or YY) entangling gate (Mølmer & Sørensen 1999; Sørensen & Mølmer 1999, 2000; Häffner et al. 2008).

## Minimal mathematical idea
- $\omega_\pm=\omega_q\pm(\omega_m+\delta)$: tones near blue/red sidebands; $\delta$ (unspecified) sets loop rate  
- $H_I\!\approx\!\hbar\eta\Omega\,(a e^{i\delta t}+a^\dagger e^{-i\delta t})(\sigma_\phi^{(1)}+\sigma_\phi^{(2)})$  
- Displacement $\alpha(t)\propto(1-e^{i\delta t})/\delta$, choose $t_g$ so $\alpha(t_g)=0\Rightarrow$ spin-motion entanglement cancels  
- Residual $U(t_g)=\exp[-i\Phi(\sigma_\phi^{(1)}+\sigma_\phi^{(2)})^2]\Rightarrow XX/YY$ entangler; choose $\Phi$ (unspecified) for desired angle
